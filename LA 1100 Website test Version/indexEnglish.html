what IS FORMEL 1?

Formula 1 (often abbreviated to F1) is a formula series authorized by the governing body of the automobile industry, the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). The racing teams involved design cars that must comply with the technical specifications set out in the Formula 1 rules. These racing cars compete in Grand Prix races (German: Große Preise) as part of the Formula 1 World Championship at currently around 20 race tracks worldwide each year. At the end of the season, the driver with the most points becomes the drivers' world champion and the manufacturer with the most points becomes the constructors' world champion. Formula 1 is the highest-ranking racing series in formula racing organized by the FIA. It is referred to as the premier class of automobile racing because it claims to place the highest technical, driving and also financial demands of all racing series on drivers and constructors. It is thus the direct successor to the former International Grand Prix Formula (French: Formule Internationale), which became Formula 1 from 1948 (with the introduction of the new Formula 2). In 1950, the Automobile World Championship was then introduced, initially only for drivers, and from 1958 also for constructors, as an overarching classification of the individual races held each season. Up to the 1959 season, races that were not held in accordance with Formula 1 regulations were also regularly included. After a fundamental reorganization of the organizational structure, the world championship has now been continued under the new official name FIA Formula One World Championship since 1981.
